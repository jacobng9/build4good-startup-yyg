1. Executive Summary
Finch is an AI-powered internship application platform built for engineering, computer science, and high-volume student applicants who are frustrated by mass-apply strategies that yield little return. Rather than automating volume, Finch optimizes for interview probability - matching students to high-signal roles, tailoring their materials per application, and increasing recruiter visibility through structured networking support.

The core product is a Chrome extension + web dashboard that connects to a student's LinkedIn profile, generates role-specific resumes and cover letters in seconds, and autofills entire ATS application forms (Greenhouse, Lever, Workday) - reducing a 20-30 minute application process to under 60 seconds. Finch stops at the final review page so the student always submits intentionally.

THE CORE PROPOSITION
Fewer applications. More interviews.

2. Problem Statement
2.1 The Student Pain Point
Students entering competitive internship markets are given two pieces of advice: apply more and network more. Neither comes with a system to actually improve outcomes. The result:
? High-volume applicants submit 100-200 applications per recruiting cycle with minimal response rates
? Applications are generic, not tailored to specific roles or ATS keyword requirements
? Students receive no feedback, face constant ghosting, and burn significant time on repetitive form-filling
? No tools exist that combine resume optimization, intelligent matching, and application automation in one workflow

2.2 Market Gap
Existing tools fall into two failure modes: they either auto-apply without optimization (producing noise, not signal), or they provide optimization without automation (requiring manual effort per application). Finch occupies the gap between these extremes - intentional, AI-optimized, and time-efficient.

4. Target Audience & User Personas
4.1 Primary User
Undergraduate students at R1 universities (initially Texas A&M) with the following profile:
? Age: 19-23
? Major: Engineering, Computer Science, or quantitative business
? Status: Actively applying for competitive internships
? Behavior: Digitally native, time-constrained, outcome-driven
? Volume: Submitting 50-200 applications per recruiting cycle with low response rates

4.2 User Personas

Persona A - "The Carpet Bomber"
A junior CS student who has submitted 150+ applications this semester with a 3% response rate. Applies to everything hoping something sticks. Needs Finch to reduce volume and increase targeting signal. Primary concern: getting any response at all.

Persona B - "The Selective Applier"
A sophomore Industrial Engineering student applying only to 10-15 top-choice roles. Has a strong resume but spends 45 minutes per application tailoring manually. Needs Finch to automate the tailoring and form-filling work while maintaining quality.

Persona C - "The First-Timer"
A freshman or sophomore who has never applied to an internship before and is intimidated by the process. Needs Finch as a guide to understand what a good application looks like and where to start.

9. Technical Architecture Overview
9.1 Core Stack
? Backend: FastAPI (Python), PostgreSQL
? AI / ML: LangChain, LaTeX resume generation, Hugging Face models
? Extension: Chrome Manifest V3, content scripts for ATS form detection
? LinkedIn Integration: LinkedIn scraping / OAuth for profile data ingestion
? Payments: Stripe (subscription + one-time bundle management)
? Frontend: React or vanilla JS; Tailwind CSS
? Hosting: TBD (likely Vercel for frontend, Railway or Render for backend)

9.2 Data Flow
5. User signs up and connects LinkedIn - profile data scraped and stored
6. AI generates rich candidate profile (skills, experience, education)
7. Chrome extension detects ATS application page
8. Finch fetches job description, runs LLM tailoring against candidate profile
9. Tailored resume (LaTeX ? PDF) + cover letter generated in < 10 seconds
10. Extension autofills form fields and uploads PDF; pauses at final review
11. User reviews and clicks submit manually
12. Application logged to dashboard with ATS alignment score

10. Key User Stories

Onboarding
? As a new user, I can sign up with my email and connect my LinkedIn profile so Finch can generate my candidate profile automatically.
? As a new user, I can install the Finch Chrome extension from the web store so I can start applying on job platforms immediately.

Applying
? As a student, when I open a job application on Greenhouse, the Finch extension detects it and offers to generate a tailored resume and cover letter for that specific role.
? As a student, I can review the tailored materials before the extension autofills the form, so I stay in control of every submission.
? As a student, the extension fills all form fields and uploads my resume PDF but stops at the final review page - I always submit manually.

Dashboard
? As a user, I can see all my generated applications in one dashboard with ATS scores, resume versions, and cover letters linked per role.
? As a user, I can search for jobs by pasting a LinkedIn job search URL or entering keywords, then choose how many roles to run.

Billing
? As a user, I can upgrade or downgrade my subscription tier at any time through my account settings.
? As a user, I can purchase a one-time bundle if I don't want a recurring subscription.
